Here are the problems identified in the migration plan:

*   **[severity high] Circular dependency between Phase B and Phase D** — The Phase B description states that Phase B cannot start until the customer-balance event schema from Phase D is implemented. However, the Cutover Checklist states that Phase D cannot start until Phase B has completed production promotion. This creates an engineering deadlock. — **Fix**: Decouple the schema definition from the service implementation. Define and lock the canonical customer-balance event schema during Phase A (Foundation), allowing Phase B to proceed independently of Phase D's deployment.
*   **[severity high] Missing Subscription Service extraction phase** — The Overview states the target architecture includes a "Subscription Service," and the Data Migration section mentions migrating "historical subscription records." However, the "Phases" section completely omits the extraction, shadowing, and promotion steps for the Subscription Service. — **Fix**: Add a dedicated phase (or explicitly integrate it into Phase B) detailing the extraction, validation, and promotion of the Subscription Service.
*   **[severity high] Data migration duplication bug** — The Data Migration section claims the migration script is "idempotent and safely re-runnable." However, the Cutover Checklist contradicts this by instructing engineers to run the migration by "appending new rows for every source record processed during the batch." Appending rows on re-runs will cause massive data duplication and corrupt financial records. — **Fix**: Update the Cutover Checklist to specify an upsert (insert or update on conflict of source ID) strategy to guarantee true idempotency.
*   **[severity med] Lack of real-time data synchronization during shadow phases** — The plan relies on batch migrations but does not detail how data mutations (e.g., a customer updating their subscription or address) are synced to the new services in real-time during the multi-week shadow phases. This will lead to validation failures due to stale data. — **Fix**: Implement a dual-write mechanism or a Change Data Capture (CDC) pipeline starting in Phase A to keep the service databases continuously in sync with the monolith during shadow testing.
*   **[severity med] Double-charging risk during Payment Service rollback** — The Phase C rollback plan states that in-flight payment intents must be reconciled, but does not specify how to prevent race conditions or double-charging if a rollback occurs mid-transaction. — **Fix**: Mandate the use of shared, deterministic idempotency keys (passed from the billing facade to both the monolith and the Payment Service, and ultimately to the payment gateway) to guarantee that a retried payment on the legacy path is recognized by the gateway as the same transaction.
*   **[severity low] Undefined financial variance tolerances** — The rollback section mentions "invoice total variance above tolerance" as a trigger, but does not define what the tolerance is. For billing and ledger systems, invoice total variance must typically be zero. — **Fix**: Explicitly define the tolerances (e.g., "invoice total variance must be exactly $0.00, and payment authorization degradation must not exceed 0.5% compared to the baseline").

***

VERDICT — don't ship, because of a critical circular phase dependency, the complete omission of the Subscription Service extraction phase, and a data-duplication bug in the cutover checklist.
